Abstract Large language models (LLMs) increasingly mediate how consumers and professionals form impressions of organisations. Most monitoring of this mediation is conducted in English, on the assumption that an English-language query returns a representative picture. This study measures how far that assumption holds. We queried three grounded LLMs (GPT-5.4, Google Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Perplexity Sonar Pro) about 66 brands drawn from eleven Northern, Baltic, and Central European home markets, in twelve languages spanning four language families (Germanic, Uralic, Baltic, Slavic), generating 35,640 grounded responses. Multilingual embeddings (BGE-M3) allow direct cross-language comparison without translation. Three results emerge within this sample.

**中文**

摘要 大语言模型（LLM）越来越多地调节消费者和专业人士对组织的印象。大多数对这种中介的监控都是用英文进行的，假设英文查询返回有代表性的图景。这项研究衡量了这一假设的成立程度。我们以跨越四个语系（日耳曼语、乌拉尔语、波罗的海语、斯拉夫语）的 12 种语言，对来自 11 个北欧、波罗的海和中欧本土市场的 3 位大语言模型（GPT-5.4、Google Gemini 3.1 Pro 和 Perplexity Sonar Pro）询问了大约 66 个品牌，生成了 35,640 条有检索支撑的回答。多语言嵌入（BGE-M3）允许直接跨语言比较，无需翻译。该样本中出现了三个结果。

First, AI-constructed reputation is language-bound: mean cross-language cosine similarity is 0.825, responses in the same language family are more similar than across families (0.844 vs. 0.820; t = 57.98, p < 0.001, Cohen’s d = 0.31), and sentiment varies systematically by language (F = 268.5, p < 0.001, η2 = 0.077), with Uralic and Baltic languages the most positive and Germanic languages, including English, the most critical. 2.2 Multilingual AI and Language Disparity The uneven distribution of languages in LLM training data is well documented. Dodge et al. [6] found English dominates the C4 corpus, with most other languages below one percent; curated multilingual corpora retain a heavy English skew [7]. The imbalance has measurable consequences for quality: identical-question benchmarks across dozens of languages show large accuracy drops in the lowest-resource ones [8, 11, 12], and parametric factual knowledge is distributed unevenly across languages and entity popularity [21–23]. For the languages in this study the resource landscape is mixed: German is among the best-resourced web languages; Swedish, Danish, Norwegian, Polish, and Czech are mid-resource; Finnish, Estonian, Latvian, Lithuanian, and Slovak are comparatively low-resource [14]. This spread, crossing four language families, lets us separate two candidate mechanisms: divergence driven by structural language distance, which would track family boundaries, and divergence driven by training-data volume, which would track resource level regardless of family.

**中文**

2.2 多语言人工智能和语言差异 LLM 训练数据中语言分布不均匀的情况已有详细记录。道奇等人。 [6] 发现英文在 C4 语料库中占据主导地位，大多数其他语言都低于百分之一；精心策划的多语言语料库保留了严重的英文偏差[7]。这种不平衡对质量产生了可衡量的后果：数十种语言的相同问题基准显示，资源最低的语言的准确性大幅下降[8,11,12]，并且参数事实知识在语言和实体流行度之间分布不均匀[21-23]。对于本研究中的语言，资源格局是混合的：德语是资源最丰富的网络语言之一；瑞典语、丹麦语、挪威语、波兰语和捷克语属于中等资源；芬兰语、爱沙尼亚语、拉脱维亚语、立陶宛语和斯洛伐克语的资源相对较少[14]。这种跨越四个语系的传播，让我们能够分离出两种候选机制：由结构语言距离驱动的发散，它将跟踪语系边界；以及由训练数据量驱动的发散，它将跟踪资源水平，而不考虑语系。
